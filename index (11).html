About the System

Built for Eket, Powered by Community

Bridging the gap between citizens and law enforcement in Ikot Udota, Eket Local Government Area, Akwa Ibom State.

The Ikot Udota Division of the Nigeria Police Force serves the Eket Local Government Area of Akwa Ibom State, which includes Ikot Udota — home to Heritage Polytechnic and a growing community. The division has demonstrated active law enforcement, recovering weapons, making key arrests of cultists, and rescuing kidnap victims across the region.

Despite these efforts, reporting crimes remains a challenge for many community members. This system provides a fast, secure, and accessible digital channel for citizens to report incidents, receive community safety alerts, and communicate with the police — reducing response time and increasing public confidence in law enforcement.

The platform was designed as a final year project at Heritage Polytechnic, Ikot Udota, Eket, Akwa Ibom State — recognizing that technology-driven solutions can transform community policing in the local government area and across Akwa Ibom State.

The system is built to work on any device — mobile phones, tablets, and computers — ensuring every resident of Eket LGA can access it regardless of the device they own.

Project Information

About This Project

ND2 Project — Computer Science Department, Heritage Polytechnic, Ikot Udota, Eket, Akwa Ibom State.

This website is the implementation component of the final year project titled "Design and Implementation of a Crime Reporting and Alert Management System", submitted in partial fulfillment of the requirements for the award of a National Diploma (ND) at Heritage Polytechnic, Ikot Udota, Eket, Akwa Ibom State.

The project addresses the challenge of inefficient crime reporting in the Eket LGA area, where many citizens lack a fast, accessible, and anonymous channel to alert law enforcement. The system leverages modern web technology to bridge this gap and support the Ikot Udota and Eket Divisions of the Nigeria Police Force.

The system is fully responsive — meaning it works perfectly on Android phones, iPhones, tablets, and computers. It was designed with simplicity in mind so that any community member — regardless of technical background — can use it to report a crime or receive a safety alert.

Project Objectives

1

To design and implement a web-based crime reporting system for Eket LGA that citizens can access from any device.

2

To provide an anonymous crime reporting channel that protects the identity of informants in Ikot Udota and surrounding communities.

3

To build a real-time alert management system that notifies community members of verified criminal activity.

4

To reduce the average crime reporting response time in Eket Local Government Area, Akwa Ibom State.

5

To demonstrate the potential of technology-driven solutions in improving community policing in Nigerian communities.
